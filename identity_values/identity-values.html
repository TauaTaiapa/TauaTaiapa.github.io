Explain a situation where you have made an ethical decision. Discuss how you weighed up the values involved in that decision, the decision you made, and how you reflect on the decision now.

When my son turned 17, he really wanted to throw a birthday party with his friends at our house. The tricky part was that they wanted to have some drinks. After giving it some thought, I decided to allow the party with parental supervision. My reasoning was that I'd rather have them here where I could keep an eye on things, ensuring they were safe, rather than having them sneak off somewhere else. We made sure to provide both alcoholic and non-alcoholic drinks, along with plenty of food. Ultimately, everyone had a good time, and it was the right choice for everyone's safety and enjoyment.

In my learning journey, these strengths—adaptability, a keen sense about people, and life experiences—serve as valuable assets. Your limitations in terms of your learning and career development.

I've faced challenges in learning throughout my life, often feeling the pressure to conform to what seemed to come naturally to others. For a long time, I struggled with self-doubt, thinking that I wasn't as smart as everyone else. This internalized belief led me to put immense pressure on myself, sometimes adopting a clown-like demeanor as a coping mechanism. Recently, I've come to a significant realization—I'm highly likely to have ADHD. Understanding this aspect of myself has opened up a new chapter in my learning journey. It explains why traditional learning methods might not have been as effective for me and why I've encountered unique challenges. Now, as I embark on learning a new course, I'm navigating not only the subject matter but also how my ADHD may affect the process. This self-awareness allows me to explore tailored strategies and accommodations that cater to my learning style. While it presents its own set of challenges, acknowledging and addressing these limitations is a positive step toward a more personalized and effective learning experience.

Learning has consistently presented challenges for me, but I've developed effective strategies to overcome them. In team projects, I've often found myself taking a more reserved role, standing in the background rather than actively participating. This tendency stems from a fear of being perceived as unintelligent. I want to contribute meaningfully, but the worry of judgment sometimes holds me back. An example of this was during a collaborative project where my instinct was to stay in the background. Despite having valuable insights or ideas, I hesitated to voice them openly. This led to a situation where my contributions, though potentially significant, were kept to a minimum. It created a dynamic where tension or resistance may have arisen due to the perceived lack of involvement. Recognizing this pattern is crucial for me. Moving forward, I aim to actively work on overcoming this reluctance, ensuring that my unique perspectives are heard and valued in team settings. It's a part of my ongoing learning journey to not only understand the subject matter but also improve how I collaborate with others effectively.

Discuss strategies you tried at the time, how effective they were, and your reflections on what other strategies you would try now and why.

At the time, my strategy for working in team projects involved taking a more reserved role, standing in the background to avoid potential judgment. While this allowed me to be seen rather than heard, it limited the extent of my contributions. The effectiveness of this strategy can be seen in the sense that I was able to participate to some degree without feeling exposed. However, it also hindered my ability to fully engage with the team and share valuable insights. Reflecting on this, I realize that this approach might not have been the most effective in fostering true collaboration and maximizing the potential of the team project. In hindsight, I understand that contributing more actively would not only benefit the project but also provide an opportunity for me to challenge my fear of judgment. Moving forward, I would explore strategies that encourage a more inclusive and open team dynamic. This could involve setting personal goals for active participation, gradually increasing my involvement in discussions, and seeking feedback from team members. By taking these steps, I aim to create a more positive and productive collaborative environment while continuing to challenge and overcome my own reservations about contributing openly to team projects.
